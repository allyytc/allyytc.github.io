In Progress — Development Sprint

Description

The redesign is deeply audience-driven — built for two distinct users with two distinct needs. For guests, the site is warm, welcoming, and easy to navigate on a phone with no prior tech experience. The navigation is priority-based — Get Help comes first, followed by Contact Us, About Us, and Donate — because that's what matters most to someone walking through the door for the first time. For donors, the donation page shifts tone entirely: clean, professional, and trust-building, without losing the center's humanity.

Accessibility is non-negotiable. The redesign prioritizes ADA compliance, multilingual support, and a mobile-first layout — because most guests will only ever access this site from a phone.

My Role

Project Lead for Design for America, directing an 11-member cross-disciplinary team through discovery, wireframing, design iterations, and front-end development. I meet directly with the CDDC team, translate their requirements into technical specifications, and handle both the design and the code myself. The site serves 300+ daily users. When the DFA term ended, I kept going — continuing the project alongside a few team members because the work isn't finished.

Problem

The Corvallis Daytime Drop-In Center — a nonprofit providing basic needs resources, peer support, and social services to unhoused community members in Benton County — has a website that makes critical information hard to find. For guests who may only have a few minutes on a phone, a site that's difficult to navigate isn't just inconvenient, it's a barrier to getting help.

Design Process

Every feedback session is collaborative. We don't come in with a finished design and ask for approval — we build alongside the nonprofit. Early sessions focused on discovery: understanding who their guests actually are, what they need the moment they land on the site, and what the staff wished the old site had done better. From there we defined two distinct audiences and two distinct design directions, which shaped every decision that followed.

Wireframes went through multiple rounds, each one presented back to the CDDC team, marked up, and revised based on their feedback. When something didn't feel right for their guests, we changed it. The dual-tone approach for the donation page came directly out of one of those sessions.

Hackathon Wins

Description

Across the world, over 1,000 people die each year just waiting for medical care inside an emergency room lobby. Most arrive with symptoms that are survivable on their own — concussions, chest pain, bruising, spasms — but over the many hours spent waiting for a room to open up, those same conditions can escalate into strokes, heart attacks, and organ failure. In a crowded ER, someone could collapse and lose consciousness with no one noticing for minutes.

ER Overwatch connects live waiting-room footage to automated collapse detection and a nurse dashboard — giving understaffed ERs real-time alerts and structured patient context before a doctor is available.

My Role

  • Frontend development — led the nurse dashboard where staff sign patients in, monitor vitals, and receive real-time alerts
  • Fall detection — explored an OpenCV-based approach before moving to Gemini video processing for collapse and loss-of-consciousness detection

Getting fast, reliable results meant iterating on how we batch and send footage: overlapping clip windows to catch events at clip boundaries, and working around Gemini token limits as frame volume scaled up.

Tech Stack

Next.js, React, TypeScript, Vite, Tailwind CSS, FastAPI, Python, MediaPipe, LiveKit, Gemini API, REST API, WebSockets, Computer Vision, rPPG, Machine Learning

Live Site → GitHub → QuackHacks 3 →

Features

  • Nurse dashboard — sign patients in, monitor vitals, and receive real-time collapse alerts
  • Fall & collapse detection — Gemini video processing and OpenCV movement analysis on live waiting-room footage
  • Remote vitals (rPPG) — heart rate monitoring streamed from the patient’s camera to the nurse view
  • AI patient chatbot — chats with waiting patients and builds structured casefiles for staff
  • Patient wait portal — checked-in patients can view their ER data and estimated wait time
View Project

Description

ComboCraft started as a late-night “what if we could fight on screen with our hands?” idea and grew into a full browser game set in the Inferno Realm. A fist casts a fireball, an open hand summons an ice shard, a pointed finger calls lightning, and chaining gestures builds combo attacks.

Players face a Demon Boss whose behavior evolves across phases as its health drops. Three difficulty settings scale the encounter from easy to hard.

My Role

  • How-to / instructions page — built the in-game guide covering each gesture, spell stats, and battle tips
  • Computer vision overlay — developed the player feed composited onto the game canvas; early prototypes used the raw webcam with the full background before moving to MediaPipe Selfie Segmentation to strip the background and isolate the player
  • Gesture classification — helped train a custom sklearn model on hand-gesture samples to improve spell recognition alongside MediaPipe Hands
  • Game engine — built core JavaScript canvas systems for boss phases, spell rendering, combo attacks, and UI effects

Getting the overlay to feel natural meant iterating on segmentation quality and frame timing so the cut-out player read clearly against the boss fight without dragging down gameplay.

Tuning gesture timing meant redesigning the classifier when early motions overlapped, and replacing 60 FPS backend polling with a smoother command pipeline so spells felt responsive in the browser.

Tech Stack

JavaScript, HTML/CSS, Python, Flask, OpenCV, MediaPipe Hands, MediaPipe Selfie Segmentation, scikit-learn

GitHub → Devpost →

Features

  • Gesture spellcasting — real-time hand motions trigger fire, ice, and lightning spells with dual-hand casting
  • Combo system — chain gestures into multi-step attacks for higher damage
  • Multi-phase Demon Boss — evolving animations, attacks, and behavior as health drops
  • Mana & orb system — collect boss energy fragments to restore mana mid-fight
  • Tutorial & UI — how-to-play guide, difficulty select, and pixel-art menus
